# Cards and Grids

Most of the time, cards are grouped into a section. They are considered as teasers and they point to a specific content deeper in the website. 

- The layout is defined by a `.container` element.
- The columns are defined by `.grid` elements.

Then, use the following classes to define the width of the columns:
- `grid grid--responsive-cols-2 gap--responsive`
- `grid grid--responsive-cols-3 gap--responsive`
- `grid grid--responsive-cols-4 gap--responsive`

# Numbers of cards per section


Rule of thumb: **Put a maximum of 6 cards per section, displayed on two rows.** 

If you have more content to show, add a link into the `.section__action` slot, and point it out to your additional content.

**Exceptions:** 
- In a hub page, you have to display the entire list of cards.
- in a carousel, you could to display around 10 cards.

# Curated lists
Curated list is a section where you have full control of the layout and the appeareance of your cards. Content is created manually and you can rythm your section with different columns and card types.

export const Template = (args, { argTypes }) => ({
  props: Object.keys(argTypes),
  components: { CardListExample },
  template: `
    <section class="section bg--secondary-50">
      <div class="container">
        <h2 class="section__title">{{ sectionTitle }}</h2>
        <CardListExample 
          v-if="row1"
          :columns="row1.columns"
          :twitter="row1.twitter"
        />
        <CardListExample 
          v-if="row2"
          :columns="row2.columns"
          :twitter="row2.twitter"
        />
      </div>
    </section>
  `,
});
